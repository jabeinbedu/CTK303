And of course... the birth of Jacob Beinborn — perfectly aligned with the beginning of the three week reign of Janet Jackson's "Escapade" over the top of the Billboard Hot 100 in the first week of March.

Fast Facts

"Escapade" entered the Billboard Hot 100 on, March 3, 1990. It was the third single from her album Rhythm Nation 1814. Written by Janet Jackson herself, along with producers Jimmy Jam and Terry Lewis.

It peaked at #1 on the Billboard Hot 100 in March 1990, also seeing chart success internationally. After 3 weeks at the #1 spot, it was replaced by "Black Velvet" by Alannah Myles. It was one of seven top-5 singles from Rhythm Nation 1814, four of which claimed the #1 spot.
